The problem: manual checks average 14.8 hours (15% take over a day) and catch only 51% of true conflicts — name variations (Bob vs Robert, "ABC Corp" vs "ABC Corporation", punctuation/casing) slip past an eyeball review. That's both a member-experience delay and a compliance risk. The fix: a normalized-matching conflict check at the moment of intake catches 99% of conflicts, clears 83% of members instantly, and routes only 17% to a human — of which ~5% of all intakes are namesake false positives that a secondary identifier (DOB or matter) resolves.
